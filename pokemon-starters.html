BULBASAUR

Bulbasaur is a grass and poison type pokemon with three evolutions.

Bulbasaur is a small, mainly turquoise amphibian Pokémon with red eyes and a green bulb on its back. It is based on a frog/toad, with the bulb resembling a plant bulb that grows into a flower as it evolves. Bulbasaur is notable for being the very first Pokémon in the National Pokédex. It is one of the three choices for a starter Pokémon in the original Game Boy games, Pokémon Red & Blue (Red & Green in Japan), along with Charmander and Squirtle.

For some time after its birth, it uses the nutrients that are packed into the seed on its back in order to grow.

It carries a seed on its back right from birth. As its body grows larger, the seed does too.

More Info on Bulbasaur

IVYSAUR

Bulbasaur evolves into Ivysaur at level 16. Ivysaur gains a significant stat boosts, including higher HP and improved offensive capabilities Early evolution allows access to stronger moves and better survivability in battles.

The more sunlight Ivysaur bathes in, the more strength wells up within it, allowing the bud on its back to grow larger.

The bulb on its back grows as it absorbs nutrients. The bulb gives off a pleasant aroma when it blooms.

More Info on Ivysaur

VENUSAUR

Evolves from Ivysaur starting at level 32. The flower fully blooms, and Venusaur becomes a large, powerful Grass/Poison-type Pokémon.

While it basks in the sun, it can convert the light into energy. As a result, it is more powerful in the summertime.

More Info on Venusaur

CHARMANDER

The starting form, a Fire-type Pokémon introduced in Generation I. It is known for the flame at the tip of its tail, which indicates its health and mood.

The evolution line of Charmander is a popular choice among Pokémon trainers, starting from Charmander, evolving into Charmeleon, and finally into Charizard. Each evolution enhances its abilities and changes its appearance, making it a sought-after Pokémon in various games.

The flame on its tail shows the strength of its life-force. If Charmander is weak, the flame also burns weakly

If Charmander is healthy, the flame on the tip of its tail will burn vigorously and won’t go out even if it gets a bit wet.

More Info on Charmander

CHARMELEON

Charmander evolves into Charmeleon at level 16. Charmeleon is also a Fire-type Pokémon and is known for its more aggressive nature compared to Charmander. It has enhanced abilities and a stronger flame.

When it swings its burning tail, the temperature around it rises higher and higher, tormenting its opponents.

It is very hotheaded by nature, so it constantly seeks opponents to battle against. Its aggression will not be quelled if it doesn’t win.

More Info on Charmeleon.

CHARIZARD

Charizard is a dual-type Fire/Flying Pokémon and is one of the most iconic Pokémon in the franchise. It is known for its dragon-like appearance and ability to fly, making it a powerful choice for trainers.

If Charizard becomes truly angered, the flame at the tip of its tail burns in a light blue shade.

It uses its wings to fly sky-high. The more experience it gains in battle, the greater the temperature at which its flames burn

More Info on Charizard.

SQUIRTLE

A Water-type Pokémon resembling a small blue turtle with a hard shell. It is one of the three starter Pokémon in the Kanto region and is known for its ability to spray water from its mouth and retreat into its shell for protection.

Squirtle’s shell is soft at birth but hardens quickly, providing both protection and hydrodynamic advantages for swimming.

The evolution line is significant in the Pokémon games, as Squirtle is a starter Pokémon in Pokémon Red, Blue, Green, FireRed, LeafGreen, and Pokémon GO.

Each evolution increases Squirtle’s battle capabilities, including higher stats and access to more powerful moves. This evolution line is consistent across the main series Pokémon games, making Squirtle, Wartortle, and Blastoise a classic and iconic trio in the franchise.

More Info on Squirtle.

WARTORTLE

Squirtle evolves into Wartortle starting at level 16. Wartortle is larger, with a bushy tail and ear-like fins, and its abilities and stats are enhanced compared to Squirtle, making it a stronger contender in battles.

Wartortle’s long, furry tail is a symbol of longevity, so this Pokémon is quite popular among older people.

It often hides in water to stalk unwary prey. While swimming quickly, it moves its ears to maintain balance.

More Info on Wartortle.

BLASTOISE

Wartortle evolves into Blastoise at level 36. Blastoise is a fully evolved Water-type Pokémon with powerful water cannons on its shell, capable of executing high-damage Water-type moves like Hydro Pump.

It deliberately increases its body weight so it can withstand the recoil of the water jets it fires.

It has jet nozzles on its shell. This impressive Pokémon uses these jets to charge toward foes with all the force of a rocket.

More Info on Blastoise.
